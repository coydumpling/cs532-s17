                                <p>On Saturday afternoon at approximately 3:05 p.m. a tornado touched down in La Plata. A fast-moving storm caused a tornado that touched down in the northern side of La Plata, according to the Department of Emergency Services, off of Rosewick Road. The storm continued up Route 5 and through St. Charles Parkway. </p><p>Diane Richardson, a public information officer for the Charles County Sheriff's Office, said the police department, along with the department of emergency services, the members of the various Charles County volunteer fire departments and the Maryland State Police all worked together to clean debris off roadways and keep them open after the storm. </p><p>"We had officers in multiple locations directing traffic because signals were out," Richardson said. </p><div id="tncms-region-article_instory_top" class="tncms-region hidden-print"></div><p>There was an emergency command center mobilized after the storm to keep citizens as safe as possible, she said. There were about 200 calls to service during the time the storm hit, she said, due to various reasons. </p><p>"Mostly debris in the roadway, trees down, lights out. We had a lot of intrusion alarms but that happens when storms hit and activate alarms," Richardson said. </p><p>There have been no reports of any injuries as of yet, Richardson said.</p><div class="tnt-ads-container text-center "><div style="min-height:250px; min-width:300px;" class="tnt-ads dfp-ad" data-dfp-adunit="/132916964/somdnews.com/breaking" data-dfp-custom-pos="fixed-big-ad-middle-asset, stf" id="fixed-big-ad-middle-asset" data-dfp-size="[300,250]"></div></div>
    <div id="tncms-region-article_instory_middle" class="tncms-region hidden-print"></div><p>Charles County Commissioner Ken Robinson (D) said the storm caused a lot of debris and downed trees. Some spots were also hit with hail from the storm, he said. </p><p>"But there were no injuries and our departments moved quickly," Robinson said. </p><p>The storm impacted the Town of Indian Head as well. A tree fell on Indian Head Mayor Brandon Paulin's family home. No one was injured, he said. Paulin was home, he said, when the wind started blowing and hail fell from the sky. </p><p>"I couldn't see out my windows. The wind and hail was coming down so hard. So my dog and myself went into the basement and then all I heard was a boom," Paulin said. "Thank God the rest of the family was in the car down at 'Create our Community day' packing up ... I'm just happy everyone was safe."</p><div id="tncms-region-article_instory_bottom" class="tncms-region hidden-print"></div>
