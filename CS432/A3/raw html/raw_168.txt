<p>A man interviewed by Bill O&#8217;Reilly of Fox News this week, who was identified in an on-screen caption as a &#8220;Swedish Defense and National Security Advisor,&#8221; turns out to be entirely unknown in his native country, with no connections to either the nation&#8217;s defense or security services.</p>
<p>As the Swedish newspaper <a href="http://mobil.dn.se/nyheter/varlden/fake-sweden-expert-on-fox-news-has-criminal-convictions-in-us-no-connection-to-swedish-security/">Dagens Nyheter reported</a> on Friday, Nils Bildt, who echoed President Donald Trump&#8217;s debunked claim that immigrants from Muslim majority nations had driven a rise in violent crime in Sweden, has no known expertise in national security, and has not lived in his homeland since 1994. Officials at the Swedish Defense Ministry and Foreign Office told the newspaper they have never heard of this &#8220;unknown Bildt.&#8221;</p>

<p>His only claim to fame appears to be the fact that his father, Sven Tolling, is &#8220;well know in Swedish equestrian circles,&#8221; according to Dagens Nyheter. How he came to be presented by Fox News as an expert in a segment broadcast on Thursday night remains a mystery &#8212; as does why he changed his name from Tolling to Bildt after he emigrated the United States.</p>
<p>Nevertheless, when Bildt was asked by O&#8217;Reilly to respond to <a href="https://twitter.com/daccraft/status/834999373331312640">comments from Anne-Sofie Näslund</a>, a U.S. correspondent for the newspaper Expressen who disputed the Fox News host&#8217;s claim that a recent rise in violent crime in Sweden was caused by an &#8220;influx&#8221; of refugees from Muslim nations, he confidently dismissed her fact-driven argument.</p>
