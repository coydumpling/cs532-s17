		<div itemprop="articleBody">
	<p>The developer of Trump International Tower and Hotel is suing a local construction company, claiming that shoddy work and poor management of trade contractors delayed the high-profile project.</p>
<p class="p1">Holborn Developments (West Georgia) Ltd. filed a notice of civil claim on Jan. 17 in B.C. Supreme Court with a list of over 25 complaints about Richmond-based Urban One Construction Management.</p>
<p class="p1">These included failure to establish a project schedule and properly supervise trade contractors. The claim said Urban One allowed sub-par work to pass, did not&nbsp;inspect&nbsp;appliances to make sure they were in good condition and misled Holborn about possible schedule delays.</p>
<p class="p1">According to the claim, the two companies entered a construction management contract&nbsp;in July 2012.</p>
<p class="p1">None of the allegations have been proven in court.</p>
<p class="p1">In an email,&nbsp;<span class="s1">Urban One Construction Management Inc. said it was &#8220;founded five years ago and is responsible for managing some of the highest-profile construction developments in Western Canada.&nbsp;Urban One stands behind the quality of our services, reputation and people. There is no substance or merit behind the allegations identified in the lawsuit filed by&nbsp;Holborn Developments (West Georgia) Ltd. and we intend to vigorously defend against these claims.&#8221;</span></p>
<p>The tower was supposed to open last summer, but, due to construction delays, residents only moved into the condo part of the tower recently.</p>
<p>The hotel part is slated to open soon, according to Holborn, which didn&#8217;t give a specific target date.</p>
<p>The City of Vancouver said Holborn has completed a final inspection and is working &#8220;to resolve any minor deficiency&#8221; in order to secure occupancy permits.</p>
<p>Holborn is asking for unspecified damages for breach of contract, negligence and construction delays. Holborn CEO Joo Kim Tiah did not reply to queries by deadline.</p>
