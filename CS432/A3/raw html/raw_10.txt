								<div class="article__content">
									<p class="article__content article__content--intro">PM Theresa May plans a three-year &#8220;soft war&#8221; to kill Labour off. She will target more seats after the Tories’ Copeland by-election triumph.</p>
<p>She wants to launch a sustained offensive up to 70 of their most vulnerable seats to finish off Jeremy Corbyn’s wounded party.</p>
<figure class="article__media">
	<div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="0">

		<a href="#"><img class="wp-image-2947734 size-thesun-article-image" alt="Theresa May hailed the victory in Copeland yesterday" src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/theresamaycopeland.png?strip=all&amp;w=960" width="960" height="473" /></a>

				<div class="article__credit">
			Sky News		</div>
		
				<div class="article__gallery-count theme__background-color-rgba-80">
			<a href=""><span class="article__gallery-count-value theme__copy-color">5</span></a>
		</div>
			</div>

		<figcaption class="article__media-caption">
		Theresa May hailed the victory in Copeland yesterday	</figcaption>
	</figure>

<p>Some ministers have urged her to call a snap poll in the wake of her <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/news/2944135/jeremy-corbyn-denies-that-he-is-to-blame-for-labours-historic-defeat-in-copeland/">historic by-election victory in Labour-held Copeland</a>.</p>
<p>But the PM has told aides she wants to exploit the party leader’s weakness by “slowly turning the screw” from now to 2020.</p>
<p>Tory strategists believe she can snatch up to 70 seats from Labour at the next general election – banishing them from power for decades.</p>
<figure class="article__media">
	<div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="1">

		<a href="#"><img class="wp-image-2946304 size-thesun-article-image" alt="Jeremy Corbyn says he is NOT to blame for disastrous of Copeland loss" src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/promo312204543.jpg?strip=all&amp;w=960" width="960" height="540" /></a>

		
				<div class="article__gallery-count theme__background-color-rgba-80">
			<a href=""><span class="article__gallery-count-value theme__copy-color">5</span></a>
		</div>
			</div>

		<figcaption class="article__media-caption">
		Jeremy Corbyn says he is NOT to blame for disastrous of Copeland loss	</figcaption>
	</figure>

<p>A senior source said: “Theresa scented blood after Thursday’s stunning success – now she wants to go in for the kill.</p>
<p>“She’s resisting temptation to call an election now and is going for a slow death.</p>
<p>&#8220;The idea is to prolong Corbyn’s misery by keeping him in place and slowly digging out the ground from under his feet.”</p>
<p>Campaign teams will be sent to work relentlessly in a range of marginal seats which Tories now feel are within their grasp.</p>
<div id="thesun-ooyala-player-1qbXd5OTE6Qv2Rcd5_Oxm-tIkY4JWISp" class="sun-video-player ooyala-player" data-content-id="1qbXd5OTE6Qv2Rcd5_Oxm-tIkY4JWISp" ></div><figcaption class="article__video-caption">Theresa May celebrate s  Copeland win  after yesterday&#039;s by-election </figcaption>
<p>They include former northern strongholds of Halifax, Dewsbury, Wirral West and City of Chester.</p>
<p>Party workers will also flood Labour’s flimsy foothold in the south at Enfield North, Ilford North and Hampstead and Kilburn.</p>
<p>Some of them have not been held by the Tories since the early 1980s.</p>
<p>A party insider said: “It’s clear that traditional Labour voters are disillusioned with Labour’s lame duck leader.</p>
<p>“The plan is to devote time and energy in these areas to drive home the point that we are the real party of working people.”</p>
<figure class="article__media">
	<div class="article__media-img-container open-gallery" data-index="2">

		<a href="#"><img class="wp-image-2899777 size-thesun-article-image" src="https://www.thesun.co.uk/wp-content/uploads/2017/02/nintchdbpict000251798539-e1487462824959.jpg?strip=all&amp;w=698" alt="Jeremy Corbyn" width="698" height="960" /></a>

				<div class="article__credit">
			Getty Images		</div>
		
				<div class="article__gallery-count theme__background-color-rgba-80">
			<a href=""><span class="article__gallery-count-value theme__copy-color">5</span></a>
		</div>
			</div>

		<figcaption class="article__media-caption">
		Mr Corbyn insists he will lead the party into the 2020 election	</figcaption>
	</figure>

<p>The “slow death” strategy is being drawn up <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/news/2943706/copeland-by-election-result-labour-tories-jeremy-corbyn/">after Tories took Copeland, a safe Labour seat since 1935</a>, reversing Labour’s 2,564 majority at the last election.</p>
<p>It comes as a poll revealed nearly a third of voters would be more likely to back Labour if <a href="https://www.thesun.co.uk/who/jeremy-corbyn/">Mr Corbyn</a> was replaced.</p>
<p>A whopping 34 per cent of Labour supporters admit they would be more likely to stick with the party if he went, according to the ComRes survey.</p>
<p>Six in ten Lib Dem voters and a quarter of Ukip supporters also say they would consider backing Labour with a different boss – but Mr Corbyn insists he will lead the party into the 2020 election.</p>
<div id="thesun-ooyala-player-pkcXN5OTE6jwbFewLhrPyToc9z-1vvTu" class="sun-video-player ooyala-player" data-content-id="pkcXN5OTE6jwbFewLhrPyToc9z-1vvTu" ></div><figcaption class="article__video-caption">Jeremy Corbyn says he is NOT to blame for disastrous of Copeland loss</figcaption>
<p>Yesterday deputy leader Tom Watson urged him to take a "long hard look” at himself in the wake of Thursday’s historic defeat.</p>
<p>He said Labour should be looking to gain 100 seats at the next election rather than struggling to retain ones they hold.</p>
<p>But he stopped short of calling for Mr Corbyn’s to stand down, saying “this is not the time for a leadership election”.</p>
<p>The veiled criticism of the party’s direction came as Mr Watson addressed delegates at a Scottish Labour conference.</p>

<p>Mr Watson said: “I want to be clear about this week’s by-elections. I’m not going to sugar coat the results. You deserve better than that.</p>
<p>“I’m glad we won in Stoke. Gareth Snell will be a great MP, and sending Paul Nuttall back home with his tail between his legs is exactly what he deserved.</p>
<p>“But I’m hugely disappointed that Gillian Troughton won’t be joining him in Parliament as the MP for Copeland.</p>
<p>“That means that all of us with leadership roles in the Labour party need to have a long, hard look at ourselves and what’s not working.</p>
<p>“Seven years into a Tory government, we shouldn’t be facing questions about whether we can retain the seats we already hold.</p>
<p>“Our job at the next election is to gain over 100 seats. Keeping what we have is supposed to be the easy bit.”</p>

<p>Mr Watson – who attempted to negotiate Mr Corbyn’s departure during a summer coup last year – fell short of calling for the Labour leader to go.</p>
<p>“I’ve said it a lot recently. This is not the time for a leadership election. That issue was settled last year,” he said.</p>
<p>“But we have to do better. We cannot sustain this level of distance from the electorate, from our natural supporters.”</p>
<div id="thesun-ooyala-player-dzdXJ5OTE6E6JdidIbD3k33AlqfaGtNz" class="sun-video-player ooyala-player" data-content-id="dzdXJ5OTE6E6JdidIbD3k33AlqfaGtNz" ></div>
<p>David Miliband said the shift to the Left under Corbyn was “a mistake” that won’t solve Britain’s problems.</p>
<p>The former Foreign Secretary – pipped in the 2010 Labour leadership battle by his brother Ed – said he would have joined 52 Labour MPs who rebelled against his backing for the Brexit Bill.</p>
<p>He added: “This isn’t just an electability question. It’s a question of substance.</p>
<p>“I think one can achieve more radical and substantive change through a different set of positions.”</p>
