    function getUrlParams(str) {
        var vars = {}, hash;
        if (!str) return vars;
        var hashes = str.slice(str.indexOf('?') + 1).split('&');
        for(var i = 0; i < hashes.length; i++) {
            hash = hashes[i].split('=');
            vars[hash[0]] = hash[1];
        }
        return vars;
    }

<span style="color:#000000">Wow! So many to choose from. I think that for this time, I’ll choose Middle Name Pride Day on March 10<sup>th</sup>.<br>
<br>
Believe it or not, Marie is actually my middle name. When I chose my pen name, the one I’d stick with from now on during my author career, I knew I needed something that felt right. Marie was familiar enough. My mother gave me her middle name, and her mother was inspired by an aunt for my mom’s name. And as for Lavender? That was a family name as well, the maiden name of my Cherokee great-great grandmother. The name was unique, yet familiar, something that made me feel a little closer to my roots.<br>
<br>
I know some of us have first names, even middle names, which we’ve never been completely satisfied with. But why not take a day and learn about your middle name? Maybe it came from another relative. Perhaps your parents were inspired by an event they experienced, or even someone they met. Why don’t you ask them? Or at least find someone – another family member or friend – who knows why you were named that way.<br>
<br>
I love learning about my family history. If I had a lot more free time, I’d definitely be a genealogy freak. Heck, until a few years ago, I never even knew I had strong Irish roots. I was always fascinated with the culture, yet I didn’t realize there was a deeper reason. The only way you’ll discover these tidbits, though, is to find out.<br>
<br>
Who knows? Perhaps you’ll learn an interesting story that goes along with your middle name, something to carry with pride. So, why not try? You might be pleasantly surprised.<br>
<br>
Well, I guess that time has come once more. For now, I’ll sign off.<br>
