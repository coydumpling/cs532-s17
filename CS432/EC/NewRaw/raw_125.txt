		<h1 class="entry-title">Lavender Better Than Placebo for Migraine &#8211; Chiropractic News by ChiroNexus</h1>

				<div class="entry-meta">
			<span class="sep">Posted on </span><a href="https://drnotley.com/lavender-better-than-placebo-for-migraine/" title="6:06 pm" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date" datetime="2014-02-04T18:06:05+00:00">February 4, 2014</time></a><span class="by-author"> <span class="sep"> by </span> <span class="author vcard"><a class="url fn n" href="https://drnotley.com/author/DrNotley/" title="View all posts by DrNotley" rel="author">DrNotley</a></span></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

	<div class="entry-content">
		<p><a href="http://drnotley.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Understanding-pain.png"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-495" alt="Understanding pain" src="http://drnotley.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Understanding-pain.png" width="248" height="44" /></a>One of the problems I love treating are headaches.  Cervicogenic headaches, headaches that are a result of problems with the neck joints or muscles, are often successfully treated with spinal manipulation (&#8220;cracking joints&#8221;), Active Release Techniques, acupuncture and exercise. Treating people for migraines, on the other hand, is a little trickier.</p>
<p>In the research, there seems to be little benefit of chiropractic manipulation during an acute migraine.  There is evidence that it can help reduce the severity and frequency of subsequent migraines.  Acupuncture has also been shown to help reduce pain during an acute migraine.  Sadly, when a person has a full blown migraine they will likely not be wanting to leave the house and come to the office.  Acute sufferers will want to stay still in a quiet, dark room.  So how can a person help with their migraines if they do not use medications?  I found this interesting research paper that may be a benefit for acute migraine suffers.  Check it out</p>
<p><a href="http://www.chironexus.net/2014/02/lavender-better-placebo-migraine/">Lavender Better Than Placebo for Migraine &#8211; Chiropractic News by ChiroNexus</a>.</p>
<h2>You can also check out some of the research on migraines/headaches</h2>
<p><a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9798179" target="_blank">The efficacy of spinal manipulation, amitriptyline and the combination of both therapies for the prophylaxis of migraine headache.</a></p>
<p><a href="http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed?term=Bronfort%20G%5BAuthor%5D&amp;cauthor=true&amp;cauthor_uid=7790794" target="_blank">Spinal manipulation vs. amitriptyline for the treatment of chronic tension-type headaches: a randomized clinical trial.</a></p>
